## 6. Execution Layer: Model Selection & Trade-offs

### The Expanded Framework (With Execution)

| Phase | Focus | Execution Layer |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **1. Sell Better** | Revenue & Growth | What models actually drive customer engagement? |
| **2. Improve Processes** | Operations & Efficiency | What's the cost vs. speed trade-off? |
| **3. Facilitate Decisions** | Intelligence & Insights | Do we need predictive, prescriptive, or generative? |
| **4. Map & Prioritize** | Strategy & Alignment | Which are feasible given today's models? |
| **5. Execute & Optimize** | Model Selection & Cost | What's the ROI after compute costs? |

### The Key Insight

> "An AI strategist doesn't just hand off a requirements doc. They help answer: **Should this be a fine-tuned open-source model, an API call to GPT-4, or a simple heuristic rule?** Because the answer isn't just about accuracy – it's about cost, latency, maintainability, and risk."

### Model Selection Spectrum

| Approach | Best For | Trade-offs |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Rule-based / Heuristics** | Simple, stable problems | Cheap, interpretable, but brittle |
| **Small ML Models** | Tabular data, structured problems | Good performance, low cost |
| **API Calls to Foundation Models** | Text, image, chat applications | Fast to implement, ongoing per-token costs |
| **Fine-tuned Open-Source** | Domain-specific needs | More control, requires ML ops |
| **Train from Scratch** | Novel research problems | Almost never worth it for most businesses |

### Talking Points for the Call

> "One thing I've observed from my certificate work is that it's easy to learn about AI capabilities, but hard to learn **how to choose**. When do you use a small, cheap model versus a massive foundation model? When does fine-tuning make sense versus RAG? Those decisions have huge budget implications."

> "In your work with companies, where do you see the biggest tension between what's technically possible and what's economically sensible?"

> "An AI Strategist has to be **technical enough** to understand model trade-offs, but **strategic enough** to translate those into business terms."

## 10. Full-Time Employee vs. Consultant Analysis

### The Trade-Off

| Dimension | Full-Time Employee | External Consultant |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Cost** | Higher (salary, benefits, equity) | Lower (project-based, no overhead) |
| **Depth of Business Understanding** | High – lives and breathes the company daily | Lower – limited time to absorb culture |
| **Breadth of Perspective** | Narrower – knows one company deeply | Wider – sees patterns across industries |
| **Accountability** | Long-term – owns outcomes over years | Short-term – delivers recommendations |
| **Integration** | Can drive implementation end-to-end | Often hands off to internal teams |
| **Risk** | Strategy may become insular | Strategy may become generic "one-off" |

### The Critical Risk

> "A consultant-driven AI strategy can become a **'one-off' project** – a PowerPoint deck that looks great but never truly integrates into the company's DNA."

### The Hybrid Ideal

> "The best approach might be hybrid: bring in consultants for **breadth and fresh perspective**, but pair them with internal leaders who have **depth and continuity.** The consultant shows what's possible; the internal leader makes it stick."

### Talking Points for the Call

> "I'm curious about what you see in the market. Are companies mostly hiring full-time AI leaders now, or are they still experimenting with consultants? And for those who go the consultant route, what separates the ones where AI becomes embedded versus the ones where it remains a one-off initiative?"

## 12. Module-by-Module Curriculum Explanation

### Program Overview

> *"EMPOWERING LEADERS AND INNOVATORS FOR TOMORROW"*

This tagline captures the program's core philosophy. It's not just about teaching AI tools – it's about developing **leaders** who can shape how their organizations use AI, and **innovators** who can spot opportunities others miss. The "tomorrow" part acknowledges that AI is evolving, so they're building capabilities that will last, not just skills that will expire.

---

### Module 1: The Diagnosis — Identifying Friction & Operational Leverage

**Core Idea:** AI applied to a broken process simply scales failure.

**Explanation:**
This module starts with a crucial insight: if your process is broken, automating it with AI just makes you fail faster. Before you do anything else, you have to understand where the real friction is.

The "Friction Audit" helps leaders distinguish between **"toil"** – low-value, repetitive work that drains resources – and **"high-value judgment"** – the work that actually requires human insight and creativity. AI should eliminate the first so humans can focus on the second.

**Outcome:** A Friction Log and Leverage Hypothesis – a documented list of pain points and a clear theory of how AI could create leverage.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
An AI strategist can't just chase shiny objects. They have to diagnose where AI will actually move the needle. This module teaches that discipline.

---

### Module 2: The Trust Architecture — Risk, Ethics, & Governance

**Core Idea:** Velocity without brakes is a crash.

**Explanation:**
This is one of the most important – and most overlooked – modules. When companies rush to adopt AI, they often forget that speed without safety is dangerous. This module is about building the "brakes" so you can drive fast without crashing.

Leaders learn to design a governance layer that enables innovation while protecting the company from reputational and legal risk:
- Data privacy and security
- Regulatory compliance
- Ethical boundaries
- Accountability when things go wrong

**Outcome:** A customized Governance Protocol and Risk Radar – practical tools for monitoring risk and responding before problems escalate.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
Strategists don't just propose ideas – they protect the company from the downsides of those ideas. This module builds that protective instinct.

---

### Module 3: Symbiosis — Designing the Hybrid Workflow

**Core Idea:** Don't replace the human; extend them.

**Explanation:**
This module reframes the entire AI conversation. Instead of asking "What jobs can AI replace?", it asks "How can AI and humans work together to be better than either alone?"

The "Centaur" workflow concept – named after the mythical half-human, half-horse creature – is about designing processes where AI handles what it's good at (working memory, rapid execution, pattern recognition) while humans handle what *they're* good at (strategy, creativity, curation, empathy).

**Outcome:** A "Centaur Protocol" and 10x Workflow Design – a blueprint for human-AI collaboration that multiplies capability rather than diminishing it.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
This is where the strategist moves from theory to design. They're not just identifying opportunities – they're actually designing how work gets done in a hybrid world.

---

### Module 4: Cognitive Empathy — Leading the Human Transition

**Core Idea:** Technology changes quickly; people change slowly.

**Explanation:**
This might be the most underrated module in the entire program. Companies pour millions into AI technology and then wonder why no one uses it. The answer is almost always human resistance.

This module equips leaders to manage the psychological side of AI adoption:
- Fear of displacement
- Imposter syndrome when people feel they can't keep up
- The cultural shift required to make AI truly stick

**Outcome:** An Internal Communication & Change Management Plan – a practical playbook for bringing people along, addressing their concerns, and building a culture where AI is seen as an ally rather than a threat.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
An AI strategist who ignores the human layer is setting themselves up for failure. This module builds the empathy and communication skills needed to actually make change happen.

---

### Module 5: Decision Architecture — Authority & The Explanatory Gap

**Core Idea:** Who is responsible when the machine is wrong?

**Explanation:**
This module tackles one of the thorniest questions in AI: accountability. When a human makes a bad decision, we know who to hold responsible. When an AI makes a bad decision – or worse, when a human relies on an AI and makes a bad decision – who's at fault?

The "Decision Matrix" helps leaders clarify exactly which decisions are:
- **Fully delegated to AI** (let the machine decide)
- **Shared** (human and AI collaborate)
- **Human-owned** (AI informs, but human decides)

**Outcome:** A Decision Rights Chart and "Circuit Breaker" protocols – clear boundaries and emergency stops when things go wrong.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
Strategists have to design not just *what* AI does, but *who's responsible* when it does it. This module builds that governance thinking.

---

### Module 6: The Intelligence OS — Operationalizing the Strategy

**Core Idea:** Moving from ad-hoc experiments to systemic infrastructure.

**Explanation:**
Many companies treat AI as a series of experiments – a chatbot here, a prediction model there. But real competitive advantage comes when AI becomes part of the company's operating system.

This module covers the architecture of an "Intelligence Operating System" (IOS): the data hygiene, interoperability, and system-wide intelligence that makes AI work at scale. It's about moving from one-off projects to an infrastructure that supports AI everywhere.

**Outcome:** An Intelligence OS System Map – a visual blueprint of how data flows, where models live, and how intelligence propagates across the organization.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
This is where the strategist thinks like an architect. They're not just picking projects – they're designing the foundation that makes all future AI work possible.

---

### Module 7: The Roadmap — Execution, Iteration, & KPIs

**Core Idea:** How do we measure success?

**Explanation:**
It's surprisingly easy to build AI that looks impressive but delivers no real business value. This module is about measuring what actually matters.

Leaders learn to move beyond "vanity metrics" – like number of models deployed or API calls made – to define **I-ROI (Intelligence Return on Investment).** How does this AI actually impact revenue, costs, or customer satisfaction?

**Outcome:** A 90-Day Rollout Plan and KPI Dashboard – a practical, time-bound plan for taking AI from pilot to scale, with clear metrics for success at each stage.

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
Strategists don't just propose – they execute. This module gives them the tools to turn vision into a concrete plan with accountable outcomes.

---

### Module 8: Capstone — The Board-Ready Defense Synthesis

**Core Idea:** Assemble everything into a coherent, defensible strategy.

**Explanation:**
This is where it all comes together. Leaders take every artifact they've built – the Friction Log, the Governance Protocol, the Centaur Workflow, the Decision Rights Chart, the Intelligence OS Map, the 90-Day Rollout Plan – and synthesize them into a single, compelling narrative.

But this isn't just an academic exercise. The strategy is designed to be "Board-Ready" – meaning it can be pitched to a Board of Directors or C-Suite leadership with confidence and clarity.

**Outcome:** The Intelligence OS Blueprint and Executive Defense – a complete package that answers every question a skeptical executive might ask: Why this? Why now? What's the risk? What's the return? How do we know it will work?

**Why it matters to an AI strategist:**
This is the moment the strategist becomes a leader. They're not just recommending – they're defending their vision to the people who control resources and direction.

---

### The Capstone Project: Your Strategic AI Plan

Throughout all eight modules, leaders aren't just learning abstract concepts – they're applying them to their **own organization's challenges.** They document their vision, goals, insights, and designs in a living document that grows with them.

By the end, they don't just have a certificate – they have a **customized Strategic AI Plan** ready to implement.
